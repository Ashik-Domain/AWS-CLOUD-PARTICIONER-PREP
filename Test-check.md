## üéØ My Exam Experience

> **Note:** In accordance with AWS exam policies and NDA, I cannot share specific exam questions or content. The following reflects my general preparation experience only.

### Preparation Timeline
- **Total Preparation Time:** ~1 year of learning AWS concepts
- **Intensive Study Period:** 1 week of focused revision before exam
- **Daily Study Time:** Varied based on schedule

### Study Approach
1. **Foundational Learning**
   - Completed AWS Cloud Practitioner Essentials course
   - Practiced with AWS Skill Builder
   - Hands-on practice with AWS Free Tier services

2. **Structured Note-Taking**
   - Created module-wise notes (Modules 1-13)
   - Organized exam domain summaries
   - Built quick reference guides

3. **Final Week Strategy**
   - Focused on revision rather than new content
   - Reviewed exam domain notes daily
   - Used flashcards for key concepts
   - Practiced with official practice questions

### Exam Day Tips
- ‚úÖ Arrive early (or log in early for online proctored exams)
- ‚úÖ Read each question carefully - keywords matter
- ‚úÖ Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
- ‚úÖ Use the flag feature for questions you want to review
- ‚úÖ Manage your time - don't spend too long on any single question
- ‚úÖ Trust your preparation and stay calm

### What Helped Most
- Understanding the **Shared Responsibility Model** thoroughly
- Knowing **which service to use for which scenario**
- Focusing on **high-level concepts** rather than deep technical details
- Practicing with **official AWS practice questions**
- Creating these notes for quick revision

### General Observations
- **Domain Distribution:** Questions fairly matched the published domain weights
- **Difficulty Level:** Fair and reasonable for a foundational certification
- **Time Management:** 90 minutes was sufficient with time to review
- **Question Style:** Scenario-based questions testing service selection and best practices

### Key Topics That Appeared Frequently
(Based on the exam guide, not violating NDA)
- Shared Responsibility Model
- IAM best practices
- Service selection scenarios
- Billing and cost optimization
- Security and compliance concepts
- AWS Global Infrastructure

## üí° Advice for Future Test-Takers

### What to Focus On
1. **Security & Compliance (Domain 2)** - Highest weight at ~30%
2. **Cloud Technology & Services (Domain 3)** - Largest domain at ~34%
3. **Shared Responsibility Model** - Appears in multiple questions
4. **Service use cases** - Know WHAT each service does, not HOW to configure

### Study Resources Priority
1. AWS Cloud Practitioner Essentials (official course)
2. AWS Skill Builder practice questions
3. AWS Whitepapers (especially Well-Architected Framework)
4. Hands-on practice with Free Tier
5. Official practice exam

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- ‚ùå Trying to memorize every service detail
- ‚ùå Skipping security topics (they're heavily tested)
- ‚ùå Not understanding billing concepts
- ‚ùå Ignoring the official exam guide
- ‚ùå Not taking official practice tests
